MinuteMind Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 14, 2026

MinuteMind is an offline-first meeting recorder and transcription app. This policy explains exactly what happens to your data — most of it never leaves your device.

What MinuteMind records

  • Audio recordings of meetings you choose to record, or files you upload.
  • Transcripts generated on-device from that audio.
  • Meeting minutes (summary, decisions, action items) generated from the transcript.
  • App preferences such as dark mode and transcription language.

Where your data stays

Audio recording and speech-to-text transcription happen entirely on your device using a bundled offline model (Whisper) — no audio is ever uploaded anywhere. Transcripts, minutes, and recordings are stored locally in the app's private storage and in a local on-device database. Exported PDFs are saved to your device's public Downloads folder so you can share them yourself.

The one thing that leaves your device

To generate meeting minutes (summary, decisions, action items), MinuteMind sends the text of your transcript only — never the audio recording — to Google's Gemini API for processing. This happens only when you choose to generate minutes from a recorded meeting.

See Google's Privacy Policy for how Google handles data sent to its APIs.

What we don't do

  • No account or sign-in required — MinuteMind doesn't know who you are.
  • No ads, no analytics SDKs, no crash reporting, no third-party trackers.
  • No data is sold or shared with anyone other than the Gemini API call described above.

Permissions

  • Microphone — required to record meetings. Only used while you're actively recording.
  • Storage — required to save exported PDF minutes to your device's Downloads folder.

Your control over your data

You can delete an individual meeting at any time from the Meetings list, or wipe everything at once from Settings → Delete All Meetings. This permanently removes all recordings, transcripts, and minutes stored on your device. Uninstalling the app also removes all locally stored data.

Children's privacy

MinuteMind is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect data from them.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted at this same URL with a new "Last updated" date.
